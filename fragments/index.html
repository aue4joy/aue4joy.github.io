What is Aue?

With religion as faith, practice, and perhaps doctrine, Aue is an atheistic religion. The faith Aue asks for is in society, and that the practice of consistently evaluating joy & woe in society is beneficial. It considers us as a modern species extraordinarily dependant on one another.

The joy/woe dichotomy is the lens used to guide adherents. Aue strives for the peace and community of religion, without supernatural dogma. A concise base which, after deliberation, should be timeless and agreeable. It should help to ground one in materialism and understand themselves and their relationship with others.

It is authored by Patrick Bowen, and is still open doctrine, subject to minor change. If one wishes to adapt Aue itself please use a different name.

Miscellaneous information

Foundation. Aue was founded in September 2020. Since then it has fluctuated between one and two adherents.

Etymology. Pronounced /aʊ̯e/, like "ou-ay" in "ouch" and "pay", and has been chosen simply for being all vowels and sounding pleasant.

Symbol. The white band represents the short time we are conscious, black being from the start of the universe, to its continuation without us (inwards to outwards).
